6. id="how-to-read" How to Read Our Performance Reports

Before diving into our track record, here is exactly what gets counted and why — so you can evaluate results on a fair basis.

What counts as a set-up

Only publicly shared ideas with all of the following are included in the performance table: a clear directional bias (Long or Short), a defined invalidation / stop-loss level, and at least one target level.

Not included: macro overviews or sector reviews with no specific trade direction; ideas presenting multiple alternative scenarios with no primary bias; educational breakdowns without explicit entry, stop, and target parameters. If it was not a clear, directional, actionable set-up — it is not in the stats.

How risk is measured — the R-multiple

All results are expressed in units of R, the ratio of outcome to initial risk. Example: Entry $100.00, Stop-Loss $95.00 (Risk = $5.00 = 1R), Target $115.00 (Reward = $15.00 = 3R). If the target is hit: +3.00R. If the stop is hit: −1.00R. If closed halfway: +1.50R. A losing trade always costs exactly −1R when the stop is hit cleanly; a winning trade returns a multiple of that same unit. This makes results comparable across all set-ups regardless of price level.

What is 1R in dollars?

1R is a universal unit — it is not a fixed dollar amount. It represents whatever you define as your risk per trade. For example, if your risk per trade is 1% of a $10,000 account, then 1R = $100. If it is 1% of a $100,000 account, then 1R = $1,000. A result of +3.08R means you made 3.08× your chosen risk amount. A result of −1.00R means you lost exactly your chosen risk amount. The R-system lets every subscriber — regardless of account size — apply results proportionally to their own portfolio.

How stop-loss risk is handled

When a stop-loss level is hit, the trade is recorded as −1.00R. This assumes the position was sized so that hitting the stop equals losing exactly one unit of risk. Slippage, gaps, or partial fills may cause slight deviations in live trading — this is noted where relevant.

TradingView publishing policy

All set-ups are published publicly on TradingView before the trade is active. TradingView does not allow ideas to be edited or deleted more than 15 minutes after publication. This means every set-up is permanently on record exactly as it was shared — entry, stop, target, and reasoning. No retroactive changes. No hindsight adjustments. What you see is what was published.
